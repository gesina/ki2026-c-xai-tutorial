Gesina Schwalbe is assistant professor at Ulm University since Apr 2026,
and since Sep 2024 leads her own junior research group
[Correctable Hybrid AI](https://isp.uni-luebeck.de/chai).
The group investigates how concept-based explainability can be
used for model verification and correction of computer vision DNNs.
This builds upon her general research of safety assurance and
trustworthiness of AI using XAI techniques,
which she pursued throughout her PhD (2018–2022, University of
Bamberg) and postdoctoral phases at Continental AG (until 2023) and
the Hybrid AI lab at University of Lübeck (2024–2026).
She received her masters degree in mathematics at the University of Regensburg.
Currently she lectures the course Explainable Artificial Intelligence at Ulm University,
and in the past several courses on foundations of machine learning.
